Causes:

    The most common cause of heart disease is a buildup of plaque in the arteries, which can lead to a heart attack or stroke. Other risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, and family history.

Treatments:

    Treatment for heart disease may include lifestyle changes (such as exercise and diet), medication (such as blood thinners and beta blockers), and/or surgery (such as bypass surgery or stenting).

Medications:

    Common medications for heart disease include statins, aspirin, beta blockers, and ACE inhibitors.

Medical Advice:

To prevent heart disease, it is important to maintain a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise, a healthy diet, not smoking, and managing any chronic conditions like high blood pressure or diabetes.
